Food Customs and Beliefs

  • Communal Dining - Meals are shared, with multiple side dishes laid in the middle for everyone to share.
  • Hierachy at the Table - Food is served to the eldest first, and younger people haev to wait until they start eating.
  • Fermentation as Preservation - Fermented foods are believed to embody patience, transformationa and longevity
  • Seasonal Eating - Koreans believe in eating foods that balance the body with the seasons.


Traditional Foods

Bibimbap is a food created by cooking rice with various meats and vegetables mixed throughout.

Kimbap is basically Korean sushi. However, there are a few major differences. Kimbap uses rice with sesame oil and salt, while sushi rice uses Rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Another major difference is the ingredients used. Kimbap uses cooked ingredients like egg, ham, bulgogi, and pickled radish, while Sushi uses raw fish, though cooked ingredients and vegetables are also used.

Tteokboki, (which is a nightmare to spell), is comprised of small, bite sized rice cakes. This is mixed in with fish cakes, and gochujang.

Bulgogi is a dish made of thin, marinated strips of meat, usually beef.It can be served with rice and vegetables.

Hotteok is a Korean snack, which comprises of a pstry filled with different fillings, These can include, red bean paste, cinammon, brown sugar and nuts


Chuseok and Songpyeong

Chuseok is a 3 day holiday celebrated on the 15th day of the 8th month of the lunisolar calendar. This is usually during mid autumn. During the holiday, families honour theur ancestors, share a large feast with traditional foods like songpyeon and newly harvested vegetables, and participate in cultural activities.

Songpyeong is a traditional Korean snack which is made of sticky rice cake around a filling thats typically made of sweet foods, like sesame seeds, chestnuts, or red beans. They are also steamed on a bed of pine needles, giving them a unique flavour.
