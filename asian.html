Common Asian Utensils

A wok. The Asian version of a frying pan. The king of Asian cookware. No Asian household is complete without one.

A bamboo steamer. Unsurprisingly, it steams things. Perfect for things like dumplings.

A rice cooker. Unsurprisingly, it cooks rice. Another staple of an Asian household.

A tandoor oven. Perfect for cooking and smoking food.

A cleaver. A giant rectangular knife. If you have one, it automatically makes you cooler.

Wok spatula. Spatula for woks. Not much more to say here

Scoop strainer. Mini strainer. Use it to strain things.

Mortar and pestle. Used for crushing things into itty-bitty pieces. Literally every cuisine so far has one of these.

Mandoline Slicer. Used for slicing things into itty bitty slices. Again, a very common piece of machinery.





Asian Food Habits

Asian food habits range from different parts, but here are a few of the staples.

  • Chopsticks-Chopsticks are very common in places like Japan, Korea, China and Vietnam. They are used for virtually everything
  • Hands-However, in places like India, Pakistan and the Middle East, you are meant to eat with your hands, It is considered a sensory experience, feeling the temperature and texture of a dish.
  • Rice- One of the staples of Asian cooking, it is incredibly common for rice to be a side, or incorporated in the main dish, or both!





Regional Specialties

Dim sums are a Chinese dish. They resemble small bite-sized dumplings, buns and rolls.

Sushi are a Japanese dish made of fish, seafood, vegetables, rice and seaweed, wropped into a cylinder. They are then sliced into small, bit sized pieces. Im referring to traditional sushi, but in reality, sushi comes in all shapes and sizes.

Kimbap is a Korean dish made of fermented vegetables, primarily cabbage. It is not spicy by itself, but can be spiced to add that kick.

Butter chiken is an Indian creamy tomato-based chicken curry. It can be served with rice or naan bread.
