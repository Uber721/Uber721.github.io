Commonly Used Equipment

This a Kadai. It is sort of like a wok. It is a deep, round bottomed pan, oerfect for frying, sauteing, and curries.

This is a tawa. It is a flat pan. It is used to make various flatbreads.

This is a pressure cooker. It is used to cook things like lentils, beans and rice quickly. A pressure cooker works by trapping steam inside a sealed pot, which increases the internal pressure and raises the water's boiling point from 100 degrees celcius to about 120 degrees celcius. This higher temperature cooks food much faster than conventional methods

This is a handi. Its quite handy to store and slow-cook foods.

This is a chakla. It is a flat rolling board that is used to roll and create flatbreads.

This is a Sil Batta. It consists of a flat stone and a cylindrical stone roller. It is used to crush spices and herbs.

Different Food Customs & Beliefs

India has many different food customs and beliefs. These range from spiritual significance, to eating habtis.

  • Offering Food To Deities- In Hinduism, you are usually meant to offer food to gods before being eaten.
  • Eating WIth Hands- This is meant to engage all five senses.
  • Hospitality-Guests are treated with utmost respect. This means getting served first, and offered the best bits.

Different Regions in India and Common Foods There

North India

North India is found, surprisingly, in the north of India. The common foods here include Butter Chicken, Naan, Chole Bhature, which is spicy chickpeas with fried bread and more.

East India

East India is in the east of India. Common foods here include Machher Jhol a type of fish curry, Aloo Poshto, which is potato with poppy seeds and Momos, a Tibetan-style dumplings popular.


South India

SOuth India is, indeed, in the south of India. It has, Dosa & Idli, a fermented rice-lentil pancakes and cakes, Sambar & Rasam, lentil-based soups with tamarind and spices and Hyderabadi Biryani,fragrant rice with meat and spices

Indian Desserts

Indian desserts range in many shapes and tastes. They can be syrupy to puddings.

This is Gulub Jamun. It is created form deep-fried milk solids soaked in rose-scented sugar syrup.

This is Rasmai. It consists of soft cheese patties soaked in saffron milk

This is Gajar ka Halwa.Carrot pudding cooked with milk and ghee

This is kaju Katli. It is a diamond shaped chashew fudge.
