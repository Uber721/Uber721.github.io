Middle Eastern Food Habits

  • Communal Dining- Eating from shared large platters is designed to reinfroce the social and family aspects of eating together.
  • Bread- Bread is a staple part of Middle Eastern cuisine, and are often used to scoop food. Yum.
  • Hospitality - Guests are treated with great respect, and it can be rude to deny food.
  • Balanced Flavours - Food is balanced with different flavours, like tangy, creamy and sweet.


Middle Eastern Regional Specialties

These are examples of some regional speciaties in the Middle East

Falafel is a Middle Eastern street food, composed of ground fava beans, chickpeas, or both, and mixed with herbs and spices before frying.

Briks are triangular bits of pastry, with filling inside, kind of like Empanadas. Brik pastry is made by slapping a sticky lump of dough onto a hot non-stick surface in overlapping circles to produce the desired size and cooked for a short amount of time. Typical fillings include tuna, ground meat, raw egg, chicken, or anchovies garnished with harissa, capers, or cheese

Tahdig is a dish consisting of rice, and other ingredients, that is cooked in apot so the rice crispies on the outside. It is then flipped over and laid on its back, with the crispy bits exposed.

Kabsa is a combination of spiced rice, meats, and sometimes vegetables. It is kind of like a type of fried rice.

Shish Kebabs are skewers with square of meat and begetables on them. They have a a wide variety, due to the almost unlimited choices.
