Italian Food Preperation Techniques

Soffrito

The Soffrito is a base of finely chopped onio, celery and carrot sauteed in olive oil. It is used to build flavour in sauces, soups and stews.


Al Dente Cooking

Al dente cooking is when you cook pasta until its firm to the bite, not mushy like you would normally cook it. This can help enhance texture, and allows pasta to hold sauce better.


Rosottatura

Risottatura (whew that's a mouthful) is when you finish cooking pasta in its sauce, with a itty bit of pasta water. It helps the starch bind everything together.


Braising (Brasato)

Brasato is basically braising meat in wine, broth and aromatics.


Grilling (Grigliata

Grilling is primarily used for meats, vegetables and seafood. It can be done over charcoal or wood for an extra smokey flavour.


Pan Roasting

Pan roasting is, as you guessed, roasting with a pan. It is used for meats and fish to develop a golden crust. It is often followed finishing in the oven.


Simmering Sauces

Tomato sauces are generally simmered for hours to deepen flavour. Gralic, basil and olive oil are added at different stages for balance


Hand Shaping Pasta

There are many different ways to make pasta. Fresh pasta can be made with just flour and eggs


Stuffing (Ripieno)

Used in ravioli, cannelloni and vegetables like stuffed zuchinni and or peppers. It involves stuffing these objects with fillings like ricotta, spinach, meats and herbs






Italian Food Customs

A traditional italian meal follows a specific sequence:

  • Antipasto - Small bites to start
  • Primo - First course
  • Secondo - Main course
  • Contorno - Side dishes
  • Dolce - Dessert
  • Caffe - Espresso to finish
  • Digestivo - Optional liqeur
Not all meals include all these courses (that would be expensive), but this is the main structure.


Meal Times
Italians usually eat their meals later than us. Lunch usually starts at 1, with dinner starts at 8!


No Mixing
Each course is served seperately. Mixing these different courses is considered improper

Italian Desserts

There are some famous Italian desserts out there.


Tiramisu

Tiramisu is a famous Italian dessert made of layers of espresso-soaked ladyfingers (not real ones, thank god), mascarpone cream, cocoa powder, and optionally, some fruit.


Gelato

Gelato is basically the Italian version of ice cream. It has less fat and more flavour
